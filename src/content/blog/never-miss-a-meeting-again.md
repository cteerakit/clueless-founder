As someone who juggles multiple projects and responsibilities, keeping track of meetings is difficult sometimes. So I look for a way to make sure I will always be aware of the upcoming meetings and hopefully never miss one again.

## The Problem: Calendar Notifications Aren’t Enough

Google Calendar and other scheduling tools have built-in notifications, but they often fall short. The notifications either pop up too early or get lost among dozens of other alerts on my phone. Worse, if my phone is on silent or I’m deep in work mode, I might not even notice them.

## Enter Buzzkill: A Smarter Way to Get Notified

![](/images/posts/never-miss-a-meeting-again/inline-01-5d9a494ff8.webp)

[Buzzkill](https://play.google.com/store/apps/details) is a notification filtering app that lets you create custom alerts based on specific triggers. Buzzkill can actually do a lot more, but we’ll focus on meeting notifications.

Note that Buzzkill is only available on Android phones. If you are an iPhone user, you may want to explore the Shortcut app.

Here’s how I use it to guarantee I never miss an important meeting:

### 1. Setting Up a Meeting Alarm

I use Google Calendar for scheduling, and I’ve set Buzzkill to trigger a persistent, loud alarm whenever a meeting notification arrives. Unlike regular notifications that can be dismissed or forgotten, this one demands my attention.

### 2. Creating a Custom Rule for Meeting Alerts

Buzzkill allows me to filter notifications based on keywords. I’ve set a rule that detects phrases like “Meeting”, “Call”, or “Zoom” from my calendar notifications and triggers an alert that’s impossible to ignore.

### 3. Ensuring I See the Notification

To make sure I actually acknowledge the alert, I’ve customized it to:

- Play a loud sound, even if my phone is on silent
- Vibrate continuously until I dismiss it
- Show a full-screen notification instead of just a banner

### 4. Backup Reminder for Extra Security

For particularly important meetings, I set a second notification that triggers an additional reminder two minutes before the event. This way, even if I’m distracted, I get another nudge right before it starts.

## The Result: No More Missed Meetings

Since implementing Buzzkill into my workflow, I haven’t missed a single meeting (from my Google Calendar, I’m still figuring out syncing Lark calendar). It’s given me peace of mind, knowing that I’ll always be reminded in a way that cuts through distractions.
